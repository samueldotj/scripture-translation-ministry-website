Language Groups Without Scripture

This means over 500 language groups, concentrated mostly in the northern regions of India, still do not have access to the Word of God in their own language. Our calling is to serve these communities faithfully and lovingly.

Our Translation Process

Our translation work is primarily led by local Christians, ensuring the highest level of fluency and cultural understanding.

The process begins when we gather Christians who are fluent in their mother tongue. We then train these believers extensively in the principles and rigorous methods of Bible translation. Equipped with this knowledge, they translate the Scriptures, typically working from Hindi or another existing Bible version, into their own language. A critical step follows: the draft is rigorously validated by the entire community to ensure it achieves faithfulness to the original text, clarity, and natural language use. Only after this approval is the Bible printed and distributed to the local churches, successfully delivering God's Word into the hands of the people.

This process ensures the translation remains faithful to God’s Word and understandable to the people.

Project Vision: Focused & Flexible

This ministry focuses on making God’s Word available to minority language groups, translating one language at a time, aiming for the fastest possible completion.

  • We intentionally avoid building a heavy organizational structure.
  • We remain simple, flexible, and field-focused.
  • Our current translation focus is the Bhojpuri language.
