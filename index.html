Why Translation Matters

The Crisis of Extinction

Many Indian communities are compelled to read Scripture only in regional languages (Hindi, Marathi, Odia) rather than their "Heart Language." If we do not act, an entire generation may miss the chance to hear God's Word in the language closest to their hearts.

Without Scripture in their own tongue, languages gradually weaken and disappear. Recent statistics show that nearly 60 languages have vanished in the last 50 years.

Our Translation Methodology

We identify languages without Scripture and meet with communities to emphasize the importance of their mother tongue.

1

Team Selection

We identify 20–25 people (for NT) or 30–35 (for OT) fluent in both mother tongue and regional language. The team is a mix of elders (strong in vernacular) and youth.

2

Training Week

A one-week intensive training covering translation methodology. We cover all expenses: food, accommodation, and travel.

3

Drafting & Checking

Translators draft from gateway languages. They perform self-checks for key terms, followed by rigorous group checking sessions.

Workshop Overview
  • Duration: 5–7 Days
  • Participants: ~30 People (25 local translators + 5 facilitators)

Our Serving Team

Mr. Christopher Thomas

M.A., M.Phil., M.Th

Founder & Director, OSM

Christopher spent the first ten years of his professional life working in the secular field, both in India and abroad. In God’s perfect timing, he received a clear call to full-time ministry, and for the past 30 years he has served with the Union of Evangelical Students of India (UESI) in various leadership roles.

Christopher and his family lived in Himachal Pradesh for nine years, overseeing and strengthening student ministries across five North Indian states.

From 2017 to 2023, Christopher served as the State Secretary of UESI–Tamil Nadu, providing leadership to the Evangelical Union (EU) ministry throughout the state.

Since 2023, he has taken up responsibility for the Bible Translation Ministry, traveling across India to support and guide OSM ministries. Christopher and his family are currently based in Nagercoil, Kanyakumari District, Tamil Nadu.
